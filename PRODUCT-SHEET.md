# Lullawood — Product & Pricing Sheet

**Last updated:** 30 Jun 2026
**Purpose:** Define what a parent gets at each tier, what triggers an upgrade, and where Lullawood sits against the 2026 market. Source of truth for tier strategy ahead of Stripe go-live.

---

## 1. The one-line verdict

Lullawood's launch prices (**Dreamer $8.99/mo, Family $12.99/mo**) are **market-validated and sit at the low end of the "meaningful personalization" band** ($10–$30/mo). The risk is *under*-pricing, not over-pricing. Ship these two tiers as-is; the headroom is a **third "Keepsake" tier later (~$19–25)** built on audio + a physical book — not a change to the launch prices.

The single most relevant proof point in the market: a near-identical product (nightly personalized story, child's name + animal + colour, delivered by email) at **$9/mo has ~900 subscribers and clears ~$8k/mo**. That is essentially Dreamer. The model works at this price.

## 2. The tiers — what a parent actually gets

### 🌙 Tier 1 — Dreamer · $8.99/mo (or $89.99/yr)
**"One child, every night."**

What you're buying:
- A brand-new, personalised story **every night**, starring your child by name.
- **Recurring characters** — a chosen companion (Fern the fox, Oliver the owl…) who returns night after night, so the child builds a relationship, not a one-off.
- A **consistent magical world** (Lullawood) that persists across stories.
- **Stories that remember** — tonight's story knows what happened on previous nights (continuity + no repetition).

**Who it's for:** a one-child household, or a parent who wants to try the ritual properly with their first/only kid.

**The job it does:** replaces "I've read Goodnight Moon 347 times" with something new, personal, and emotionally continuous — every single night — for less than the price of one picture book a month.

---

### ✨ Tier 2 — Family · $12.99/mo (or $129.99/yr) · *Most loved*
**"Up to four children."**

Everything in Dreamer, plus:
- **Up to 4 child profiles** — each with their own name, age, companion, and **separate memory thread**.
- **Sibling co-star mode** — siblings can appear in each other's stories as characters (shared adventures, "fewer arguments").
- **Per-child bedtimes** — each child's ritual tuned to them.

**Who it's for:** any household with 2+ kids. (The UK/US average family with children skews to ~1.8–2 kids, so a large share of paying households land here naturally.)

**The upgrade trigger — and it's the strongest one in the whole model:** *a second child.* The moment a parent with two kids hits the one-profile ceiling of Dreamer, Family isn't a "nice upgrade" — it's the only plan that works for their house. That makes the Dreamer→Family jump a **structural** upgrade (driven by family size), not a persuasion exercise. This is why Family should stay the default-highlighted ("Most loved") tier: most multi-kid households self-select into it.

---

### 📖 Tier 3 — Keepsake · ~$19–25/mo *(FUTURE — not at launch)*
**"The nights you'll want to keep."**

> Not built yet. Documented here so the ladder is intentional. Maps to the P3 roadmap items (audio narration, printed keepsake books). The "$25" from early notes belongs **here**, on a premium tier — never on Family.

Everything in Family, plus the two features the market consistently charges a premium for:
- **Audio narration** — professional, warm voices (ElevenLabs-class), or eventually *the parent's own recorded/cloned voice*. This is the single most reliable premium up-sell in the category (see §3).
- **Printed keepsake books** — turn a month/year of a child's stories into a physical, illustrated book. The emotional "I want to keep this forever" artifact.

**The upgrade trigger:** *emotional permanence.* Dreamer→Family is driven by logistics (more kids). Family→Keepsake is driven by **sentiment** — the parent who has fallen in love with the ritual and wants it (a) hands-free at bedtime via audio, and (b) preserved as a physical object their child keeps. This is the highest-margin, highest-loyalty tier, and it's where the category's real money sits ($15–30).

## 3. How the market tiers (the ladder everyone uses)

Across the 2026 personalized-bedtime category, tiers ladder up in a consistent order. Each rung is a feature parents will pay more for:

1. **Free hook** — a limited free tier (commonly **3 stories/month**) to prove the magic. *(Fablino, Moshi, Calm, Fableton all do this.)*
2. **Unlimited + personalization** — the entry paid tier. Unlimited nightly stories, child as named hero. *(~$5–13/mo)*
3. **Audio narration** — professional voices / full-cast. **The most common premium up-sell.** *(Bedtime Stories' 100+ ElevenLabs voices; Fable's 30+ voices; the whole Toniebox/Yoto/FABA audio-player ecosystem.)*
4. **Rich media** — illustration, video, even musicals. *(Fable: narration + music + animated video + consistent character art.)*
5. **Physical / keepsake** — printed books, print-on-demand. *(Fable printable books; the "lifetime/keepsake" framing.)*

**Where Lullawood is unusual:** most competitors gate **memory/serialization** behind their *top* tiers (or don't have it at all — "most AI story generators forget everything between sessions"). Lullawood ships **persistent memory + recurring characters in the $8.99 entry tier.** You've put a premium-tier differentiator at the entry price. That's a strong acquisition wedge — and a sign you have room to charge more later, not less.

## 5. Reading the chart — where Lullawood wins and where it's exposed

**Wins (defensible today):**
- **Memory + recurring characters at entry price.** Almost no one else has true serialized memory; those who gesture at it (Fablino's Sagas, Fable's chapters) charge up or bolt it on. This is *the* Lullawood wedge — lead marketing with it.
- **No-signup demo.** Most rivals gate behind an app download or account. "Type a name, watch it appear, no account, no email" is a real top-of-funnel advantage — protect it.
- **Honest, simple pricing.** Two clean tiers + free trial beats Oscar's confusing "coins + subscription" and the per-story metering that "turns a nightly ritual into a metered expense." Parents punish meters at bedtime.

**Exposed (the gaps that define the roadmap):**
- **No audio.** This is the category's #1 premium feature and the biggest single gap. Toniebox/Yoto/FABA is a whole ecosystem Lullawood can't touch yet. → The anchor feature of Tier 3.
- **Text-first, no illustration in the story body.** Fable/Oscar lead with illustration. Lullawood's cover art + consistent-world framing partly covers this, but it's a visible difference. *(Note: raster/painterly art is sourced externally — Claude does site/vector/placeholder only — so illustrated story bodies are a content-pipeline decision, not a code one.)*
- **No physical product.** Keepsake books are the top-tier, highest-sentiment offering. → The second pillar of Tier 3.

## 6. Pricing verdict & recommendations

**Launch (today):**
1. **Ship Dreamer $8.99 / Family $12.99 unchanged.** Validated by the $9/900-subscriber proof point and the $10–30 personalization band. Changing them now re-specs Stripe and risks delay for no proven gain.
2. **Keep Family as the highlighted "Most loved" tier** — most multi-kid households self-select into it, so anchor there.
3. **Lead all messaging with the memory/recurring-character wedge** — it's the thing competitors don't have and the reason a parent picks Lullawood over a $2/story tool.

**Post-launch (sequenced):**
4. **Build Tier 3 "Keepsake" (~$19–25)** around **audio narration first, printed books second.** Audio is the proven up-sell; books are the loyalty/sentiment lock-in. This is where the early "$25" idea correctly lives.
5. **Consider a free/limited tier later** (e.g. a capped monthly count) only if acquisition needs it — the no-signup demo already does much of that job without cannibalizing.
6. **Annual plans are a quiet lever:** Dreamer annual ($89.99) = ~$7.50/mo effective; Family annual ($129.99) = ~$10.83/mo. Push annual at the trial-conversion moment to lift LTV and cash up front.

**Open questions to resolve before Tier 3:**
- Audio: licensed pro voices (ElevenLabs-class) vs. parent voice recording/cloning? (Voice-cloning has its own consent/safety considerations — flagged, not decided.)
- Books: print-on-demand partner, unit economics, and whether Keepsake is a subscription tier or an à-la-carte add-on on top of Family.
