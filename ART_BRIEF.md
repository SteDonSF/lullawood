# Lullawood — Illustration Brief

**Style:** premium, hand-painted golden-age storybook. References: *Goodnight Moon*,
*Winnie the Pooh*, Beatrix Potter, classic illustrated storybooks. Warm, nostalgic,
timeless. **Not** flat modern vector, not neon, not corporate.

**Palette to match the site:** warm parchment & cream, gold (lamplight), forest green,
soft moonlight blue. Night scenes are warm-dark forest/charcoal — never corporate navy
or purple.

**Audience reminder:** the images are for *parents* 28–45. They should trigger "I remember
being read to" → "I want my children to have this." Emotion over cuteness.

---

## The five slots

### 1. Hero — `hero.webp` · 1600×900 · landscape
Dark moonlit scene. A parent reading to a child; an **open storybook glowing** as the
single warm light; **woodland animals gathered at the edge of the lamplight, listening**
(fox, rabbit, owl, deer). Big moon, pines (ties to the logo).
**Safe area:** keep the left ~55% readable/uncluttered — headline + button overlay there.
Faces and the glowing book should sit centre-to-right, out from under the text.

### 2–4. How It Works vignettes — `how-1/2/3.webp` · 600×600 · square
Consistent set, like three pages from one book.
- **how-1** "Tell us about your child" — a parent and child together; warm, intimate.
- **how-2** "A story arrives each night" — a lamplit letter/envelope or a glowing window at dusk.
- **how-3** "They drift off as the hero" — child asleep, open book, a companion animal curled up.

### 5. Closing — `closing.webp` · 1600×900 · landscape
The emotional payoff for *"a story they'll remember long after they outgrow bedtime."*
An **older** child or a grown moment — bedtime remembered, the book on a shelf, a parent
alone in the doorway. Bittersweet, warm. **Safe area:** centre kept calm for the headline.

## Photography (not illustration)

- `parent-1/2/3.webp` 480×600 — real test-family photos, used only with permission.
