# Lullawood — Tier & Marketing Strategy

**Last updated:** 30 Jun 2026
**Companion to:** `PRODUCT-SHEET.md` (competitor reference). This doc answers: *what does a parent get at each tier, what makes them move up, and how do we maximise conversion + revenue?* Every recommendation is checked against independent 2026 pricing/retention research (sources at end).

---

## 0. TL;DR — the verdict

1. **Generation belongs in every paid tier.** It's the core product; gating it would be absurd. ✓ your instinct.
2. **Do NOT gate persistent memory behind Tier 2.** This is the one place your proposal would backfire. Memory + recurring characters is your *core differentiator and your retention moat* — the research is unambiguous that you never gate the feature that drives retention or that customers expect as the point of the product. Gating it makes your $8.99 tier a generic commodity generator and **kneecaps retention at the tier most people land on.** Keep memory at entry.
3. **Auto-delivery (the 7pm "it just arrives" email) is a good upgrade lever — but it's a MID-tier convenience, not a top-tier feature.** A direct competitor already auto-emails at $9 entry, so it's close to table stakes. It can't anchor a premium tier. Reserve the **top** tier for **audio narration + printed keepsake books** — those justify $19–25.
4. **Your real weakness isn't the price — it's that today's tiers gate only on *number of children*, so a single-child household has no reason to ever upgrade.** Fix that post-launch by adding *convenience* and *premium* upgrade triggers that apply to everyone, not just multi-kid families.
5. **Don't let any of this delay launch.** Ship the two capacity tiers as-is (memory intact). The expansion architecture below is post-launch work.

## 2. The rule: what to gate, what never to gate

The strongest, most repeated finding across every independent source:

> **Tier on customer *segments / jobs-to-be-done*, not on a stack of features. Never gate the core/expected feature or the feature that drives retention. Reserve genuinely premium, high-leverage, or delivery-heavy features for upper tiers.** (Flexprice, Maxio, Stackrows, Tabs, SaaS-software.org)

Mapped to Lullawood:

| Feature | Where it goes | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Nightly story generation | **All tiers (core)** | Expected. The product. |
| Persistent memory + recurring characters | **Entry tier (T1)** | Core differentiator + retention moat. Drives the habit that beats AI churn. |
| More child profiles, sibling co-star | **Mid tier (capacity)** | Scales with household size — a clean, fair value metric. |
| Automated nightly delivery (7pm) | **Mid tier (convenience)** *(or core, see §5)* | "Done-for-you" ritual. Universal lever — catches single-child homes too. |
| Audio narration (pro / parent voice) | **Top tier (premium)** | The category's #1 premium up-sell. Delivery-heavy → justifies a high price. |
| Printed keepsake books | **Top tier (premium / permanence)** | Highest-sentiment, highest-margin. The "keep forever" artifact. |

## 4. The conversion & revenue playbook (Head-of-Marketing)

Every play below is validated against 2026 subscription-app data.

### A. The no-signup demo IS your single biggest conversion asset — protect it
RevenueCat 2026: **55% of all trial cancellations happen on Day 0** — *"if you don't deliver an aha! moment in the first 60 minutes, the subscriber is already gone."* Most apps can't deliver value before the paywall. **Lullawood already does** — "type your child's name, watch the story appear, no account, no email" is the *perceived-value aha* delivered instantly, for free, before any commitment. This is rare and powerful. Never gate it.

### B. Sell the *promise of memory* during the trial, because memory's payoff is delayed
Memory is a **retention aha** (it only proves itself over several nights), not a first-session aha. So during the 7-day trial you must *show the promise* before it's experienced — e.g. on the demo/first story: *"Tomorrow's story will remember tonight."* The principle (RevenueCat / Product-Led Growth): create the **moment of value perception** early even when the **moment of value experience** comes later.

### C. Keep the hard paywall + 7-day free trial — it's the higher-converting model
RevenueCat 2026: **hard paywalls convert ~5× better than freemium at Day 35 (10.7% vs 2.1%)**, and *"hard paywalls are not scaring users away."* Don't add a free tier to "build goodwill" — the data shows it sacrifices large top-of-funnel revenue for ~1% retention a year later. Your no-signup demo already does the goodwill job without cannibalising paid.

### D. Go to three tiers and anchor on the middle ("Most Loved")
Good-Better-Best (3 tiers) is the validated sweet spot (Maxio, Stackrows: avg ~3.5 tiers). A **"Most Popular/Most Loved" badge lifts conversion to the badged tier 15–25%** via the center-stage + social-proof effect (Segment8, LiveSkillsHub). Keep Family badged and visually emphasised, and **price the middle closer to the basic tier than the premium** so the compromise effect pulls buyers up.

### E. Adding Keepsake (T3) lifts Family conversion *even for people who never buy it*
This is the strongest revenue argument for building T3 sooner. **Anchoring + the decoy/compromise effect** (Tversky & Kahneman): a high top tier makes the middle look like obvious value. Today Family is the ceiling — nothing makes it look like a deal. Introduce Keepsake at $19–25 and Family becomes the sensible compromise → **more Family conversions**, independent of Keepsake's own sales. *(Companies that surfaced a higher anchor saw 15–25% more upgrades to mid-tier — not because people bought the top, but because they stopped buying the bottom.)*

### F. Push annual at the moment of trial conversion
Annual plans **pull cash forward and reduce churn** (ProfitWell: a 20–30% annual discount is the effective band). Your current annual is ~17% off ($8.99×12=$107.88 → $89.99). Consider nudging toward ~20% and presenting annual prominently *at the convert-from-trial moment.* Caveat (RevenueCat): **>⅓ of annual subscribers cancel auto-renew within the first month** — so the onboarding must bank the habit fast regardless of plan length.

### G. Lean on memory as the *retention* story, not just a feature
Because **AI apps churn 30% faster**, your moat is the thing that makes tonight depend on last night. Market it as relationship, not technology: *"Fern remembers your walk to school."* Retention is where AI products win or lose the category — and memory is your answer.

### H. Build the referral / gifting loop (the virality aha)
Kids' content is intensely word-of-mouth ("my 4-year-old asks for it every night" → parent tells other parents). A **gift-a-month / referral** mechanic turns delighted parents into a channel and lowers CAC. (Already on the roadmap as gifting — prioritise it once live.)

### I. Lead with value, not price, on the pricing page
*"Customers who see numbers before understanding value make price-based decisions"* (Segment8). Put the outcome/feature value above the price on each card. You already do this well ("Less than a single picture book a month" + feature bullets above the figure) — keep it.

## 5. One open decision: is auto-delivery core or a mid-tier lever?

There's a genuine tension to resolve before building it:
- **Argument for mid-tier (Family):** it's the universal convenience upgrade that finally gives single-child homes a reason to move up. Classic, defensible "self-serve vs done-for-you" price fence.
- **Argument for core (all tiers):** a direct competitor auto-emails nightly at **$9 entry**, so gating delivery could make Lullawood look *less* convenient than a same-priced rival, and the brand promise ("every night, a new story") leans on it.

**Recommendation:** make it a **Family (mid-tier) benefit at launch of that feature, but message Dreamer's manual creation as a *feature*, not a lack** — "choose tonight's adventure together" (an active bonding ritual) vs Family's "it's already waiting at bedtime" (hands-free). If retention data later shows Dreamer users churning on the friction of manual creation, move auto-delivery to core and find a different mid-tier lever (e.g. multiple stories/day, premium worlds). Decide with data, not now.

## 6. Sequencing (so this doesn't fight velocity)

**Launch today — unchanged:**
- Ship **Dreamer $8.99 / Family $12.99**, capacity-gated, **memory in both.** Validated, ready, don't re-architect.
- Keep the no-signup demo, the 7-day hard-paywall trial, the "Most Loved" badge on Family, value-first pricing copy.

**Post-launch — the expansion architecture (in priority order):**
1. **Build Keepsake (T3, $19–25)** around audio first, books second — biggest single revenue lever (anchors Family up + new premium segment). Maps to existing P3 roadmap items.
2. **Add automated nightly delivery** as the Family convenience trigger (resolves the single-child upgrade gap). Message per §5.
3. **Ship referral/gifting** to lower CAC.
4. **Tune annual discount** toward ~20% and surface it at trial-conversion.
5. **Instrument the funnel** (trial-start, Day-0 cancel, trial→paid, tier mix, churn by tier) so every lever above is tuned on data, not intuition — *only 39% of SaaS firms price on value; the rest guess or copy. Be in the 39%.*
